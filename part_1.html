General project info

What is this app?

This web app is a place to connect with neighbours in your neighbourhood to find out about local events happening.

Some sample user stories

  • David is new to the neighbourhood and would like to meet up with some people. However, he is a bit shy and does not like approaching people in-person to ask if they want to meet up; instead, he wants to be able to find an online community where many of his neighbours are connected to find out about local events to attend.
  • Heather, a computer scientist, has lived in the neighbourhood for a while. When she first moved in (long before COVID), she used to attend lots of local events that would be advertised on pieces of paper attached to power poles, giving her a chance to connect with people. However, when COVID-19 came, nobody was socializing for a while, and even though more people are gathering now, the rate at which the neighbours hang out is still much less than it used to be. Heather would like to revive that, in a way where people can simply log on online. She does not want to simply set up some big online chatroom, as that does not really solve the problem of people meeting up in-person. Instead, she wants to set up a local online billboard where she and all the neighbours can advertise social gatherings and more.
  • Jeff is a friendly neighbour who does a lot for his community, such as organizing meetups, running charity events, helping in people's gardens, and much more. However, even though the people who know him really appreciate his help, he wishes he could help more people, as he figures there are lots of neighbours who could use a bit more help. By giving everyone a convenient online hub to check occasionally, he can reach out to more people easily who may need help.

For Jeff, what are his 5 needs that he can use this app for?

  • Advertising a local-firefighter charity event
  • Offering help to older people who need help cleaning their gardens
  • Doing cheaper local food delivery (cheaper than Uber Eats, for example)
  • Advertising a BBQ evening at the local park
  • Offering to salt people's sidewalks if people are away when it freezes

What is the user journey like in this app?

The user will immediately see a list of posts made about local events happening or general discussion, with a preview of the post content. When a user clicks to view a post, they will see the full content, as well as the comments section, where they can also add a comment. On the main page, the user can also create a post, with the title, the text, and a couple colour options (title/post info and outer border). However, the user will generally have a better experience if they log in with a username, which gives them the ability to edit/delete the post and have their posts/comments linked to their username. They will be able to log in/log out anytime in the top menu bar on the website.

What is the key takeaway for any user?

The key takeaway is that this web app is a simple way to find out about local events happening near you, with the digital convenience and without some big remote server running it.
